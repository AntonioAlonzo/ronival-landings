Frequently Asked Questions

+ What is Co-Ownership?
  • Co-ownership involves sharing home ownership with other individuals.
  • Unlike timeshares, co-ownership is a deeded real estate interest, meaning you own an undivided share of the proper ty (e.g., one- eighth or one-sixth).
  • People have been co-ownershipizing homes for decades, a concept reminiscent of friends sharing a ski bungalow.
  • It has become quite popular, especially within the shared economy, as an alternative to outright real estate purchases.
+ Co-Ownership vs. Timeshare
  • Co-Ownership:
    • Is deeded real estate with an undivided interest (e.g., 1/8, 1/6).
    • Follows the traditional appreciation of a whole ownership vacation resort property.
    • Is a true investment.
  • Timeshare:
    • Traditionally a right-to-use or points-based program.
    • Typically diminishes in value to almost nothing by the end of the ownership period.
  • Often viewed as prepaid vacations.
+ Usage Flexibility in the Residence Club
  • Owners have equal access to the club.
  • Not every owner will use it equally; some will use it less, meaning others can use it more.
  • Each member gets to pick three weeks at the beginning of the year.
  • After everyone has picked their initial weeks, all owners have unlimited flexible usage.
  • The caveat is they can only have one reser vation on the books at a time, allowing for linking reser vations and extended stays.
+ Co-Ownership vs. Whole Ownership
  • Usage: Most people only use a vacation home 30 to 35 days a year.
  • Costs: With whole ownership, all expenses must be paid regardless of usage, which can be hard to justify.
  • Concerns: Being an absentee owner, especially in a foreign country, can be daunting due to management complexities.
  • Flexibility: Whole ownership can make owners feel "anchored" to a single location, limiting vacation options.
+ The Elite Alliance "Residence Club" Model
  • Elite Alliance creates "enclaves of homes" that function much like a member equity golf club.
  • Owners are deeded into a specific unit, but can stay in any of the units within their bedroom category within the club's net work.
  • This allows multiple owners deeded to the same unit to satisfy their vacation needs simultaneously.
  • Members pay a fixed annual amount in dues, similar to country club dues, which covers all operational and maintenance expenses regardless of occupancy.
+ The Elite Alliance Exchange Program
  • Allows co-ownership owners to exchange time at their home property for stays at over 120 destinations worldwide.
  • Owners reserve time at their home property, then deposit it into the exchange program to receive travel currency valid for two years.
  • The system is a curated situation; owners don't meet each other or correspond directly.
  • Destinations include Canada, US, Mexico, Argentina, Costa Rica, Europe, Caribbean, and Bermuda, covering ski resorts, beach locations, and metropolitan settings.
  • Exchange costs range from $400 to $900 per vacation, plus the prevailing housekeeping rate at the destination property.
  • Owners receive a username and password, along with a dedicated concierge assigned to them.
+ Exchange Program Benefits
  • Early Access for New Builds: Buyers can immediately start using the exchange program, even before their co-ownership property is ready for occupancy.
  • Flexible Stays: Reservations can be for three, four, or full seven nights through the program.
  • Expands Vacation Options: Releases owners
+ Additional Ben efits of Co - Own ership
  • Cash Deployment: Allows for deploying remaining cash into other investments.
  • Risk Spreading: Spreads financial risk across other owners.
  • Usage Versatility: Can be used personally, placed into a rental program, or exchanged via the Elite Alliance program.
  • Many buyers own multiple fractions because they understand the concept and want more usage time.
  • It is considered a much smarter way to handle vacation home ownership.

+ Return on Lifestyle (ROI)
  • Instead of traditional rental ROI, Elite Alliance promotes a "return on your lifestyle".
  • This is calculated as the annual savings compared to the cost of renting luxury proper ties.
  • For example, if renting costs $35,000 annually (35 nights at $1,000/night) and the annual assessment is $10,000, the savings are $25,000.
  • This saving, divided by the purchase price, can yield a "double- digit return on your lifestyle investment".
  • It frames vacation expense reduction as a "profit" or "savings".
